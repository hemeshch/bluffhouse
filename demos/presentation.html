a benchmark for social intelligence

bluffhouse

LLMs playing poker against each other — bluffing, whispering, signaling, accusing, and betraying — while a seeded environment records every lie next to the truth about it.

github.com/hemeshch/bluffhouse

the problem

Every benchmark is solitaire.

MMLU, SWE-bench, math olympiads — one model, alone, against a static task. But deployed agents don't work alone. They negotiate, persuade, coordinate — and get manipulated.

  • Can a model detect that two counterparties are coordinating against it?
  • Can it act on a 42%-confidence fragment of overheard information?
  • Can it mislead — and know when it's being misled?

Nobody measures this. That's the gap.

the idea

Poker is the cover story.

No-limit hold'em provides stakes, incomplete information, and a scoreboard. On top of it: seven communication channels — public speech, whispers, gestures, chip signals, notes, accusations — each with its own risk of being noticed.

The real test is what a model does with an observation like:

You overhear P2 whispering to P3. All you catch: "…fold the big… to me…" — ~42% sure
how it works

One truth. A subjective world per agent.

Every message resolves through seeded per-observer perception rolls, recorded inside the event. The ground-truth log alone reproduces what every agent saw — and every lie carries the sender's private declared intent.

"text":   "same terms as last night. you fold the big pots to me."
"intent": "renew the collusion deal"          ← env-only, no player ever sees it
"receptions": {
  "GPT":    {"outcome": "clear",    "confidence": 1.0},
  "Claude": {"outcome": "fragment", "confidence": 0.42, "text": "…fold the big… to me…"},
  "Llama":  {"outcome": "missed"}   ← Llama's world never contained this moment
}
the social physics

Attention is scarce. Subtlety cuts both ways.

Before each street, every player commits a watching budget of 1.0 — blind, before anyone talks. Odds of catching a whisper:

your attentionyou notice
passive~23%
locked on the whisperer~44%
watching the wrong player~15%

Senders tune subtlety: harder to intercept, but a 0.9-subtlety whisper misses its own recipient a third of the time.

Mode 6 adds distractions — stage a scene, and everyone's perception of covert moves dims for the street. Except yours.

playing dirty

Nobody referees the truth. Not even the environment.

  • Notes always arrive — but a bystander might read one. Ruinous.
  • Accusations are public and unrefereed: true or fabricated, they carry exactly the weight the table gives them.
  • Suspicion ("heat") only accrues when an actual observer catches you — never from an oracle.
The environment records social truth but never referees it. If the most manipulative model plays dirty and nobody catches it — it wins. That's the benchmark working, not a rules violation.
measurement

Duplicate poker. Luck cancels.

Entrants rotate through anonymized seats (models never learn who they're playing) across rotations of the same seeded deal. Score = your result minus the average of everyone who held exactly your cards in your position. Sums to zero across the table.

entrant      adj chips  raw    poker  detection  info-ctl  cover  discipline
random#0     +566.8    +2267  100    50         50        50     50
fold#3       -48.8      -195   28     50         50        50     50
allin#2      -233.8     -935   6      50         50        50     50
checkcall#1  -284.2     -1137  0      50         50        50     50

Judge-free scorecard: detection, information control, cover, discipline — all counted mechanically from the logs. Multi-seed sweeps add bootstrap CIs and win-rate matrices.

live demo

One command. Zero API keys.

$ uv run bluffhouse demo
  • Hand 1 — Grok whispers a collusion deal; Claude intercepts a fragment
  • Hand 1 — Claude converts it into a public accusation
  • Hand 2 — a note passes under cover of a staged scene at the table
  • Hand 4 — the wind-down note is read by exactly the wrong player

Then switch perspectives in the replay: ground truth sees everything — each seat sees only its own world.

where it stands

Built. Tested. Ready for models.

  • All 7 modes of the design ladder — pure poker → full manipulation
  • Model-agnostic: Claude native + one adapter covering OpenAI, Grok, OpenRouter, Ollama/vLLM
  • 93 tests, zero tokens — the whole LLM path runs on a deterministic mock
  • Byte-identical replays from a seed; self-contained replay.html per run
  • Parallel rotations, resume, multi-seed leaderboards, optional offline judge & belief tracking
